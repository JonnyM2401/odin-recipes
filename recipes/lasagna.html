Ingredients

For the Bolognese:

  • olive oil
  • 500g minced beef
  • 1 onion
  • 2 clove of garlic
  • parsley
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 can of peeled tomatoes,
  • red wine

For the béchamel sauce:

  • 0.5L milk
  • 30g butter
  • 40g flour
  • salt and pepper
  • lemon juice
  • nutmeg

Extra:

  • fat
  • 300g lasagna plates
  • grated cheese
  • butter flakes

Preparation

  1. Ragú Bolognese:
    Heat the olive oil in a saucepan, brown the ground beef all over, and add the chopped onions and parsley. Stir in the finely sliced garlic and tomato paste and fry. Pour in the canned tomatoes, season with salt and pepper. Add red wine, if desired. Let the ragú simmer uncovered for at least half an hour.
  2. Béchamel sauce:
    Melt the butter in a small saucepan and whisk in the flour, stirring until light yellow. Pour in the milk and whisk until smooth. If you stir too slowly and find lumps in the sauce, strain the sauce through a fine sieve and then continue to cook. The sauce should simmer on low heat for about half an hour to remove the flour flavor. Season to taste with salt, pepper, lemon juice, and a little nutmeg.
  3. Prepare the lasagna:
    Spread some Bolognese ragú in a buttered, ovenproof dish. Place a layer of lasagna sheets on top. Cover the pasta layer with another layer of ragú, then a layer of béchamel. Then add another layer of pasta, ragú, and béchamel. Layer by layer, filling the dish. The final layer should be the béchamel sauce. Sprinkle generously with grated cheese and dot with butter.
  4. Bake the lasagna in a hot oven at 180°C for about 30-40 minutes until the crust is golden brown.
